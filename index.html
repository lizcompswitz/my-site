Coaches
Box.

Reimagining the coaches box.

At college and elite track and field meets, coaches are asked to stand in designated areas during competition called a coaching or coaches box. While the intent is to keep everyone safe and mitigate distractions, the reality is that these spaces are often uncomfortable and poorly placed.

Most stadiums are not orginally designed with these spaces in mind, so coaches end up watching their athletes from awkward angles with no place to sit while constantly barked at by officals to "get in the box." As a college pole vault coach, I have experienced the frustration of trying to effectively coach my athletes from these suboptimal areas.

In an effort to reclaim the space, at least metaphorically, I created Coaches Box (the digital tool) to be the exact opposite of coaching boxes (the physical spaces).

Finally, a coaches box worth standing in.

Coaches Box is a tool that gives structure for a sport that lives in and outside the lines. It's a space for track and field coaches to build and evolve their training programs, track their athletes' progress, capture program goals, and, ultimately, create a better athlete experience. Built from the ground up by a coach who likes to experiment but stay organized, Coaches Box makes it easier than ever to create and sustain effective training programs.

Pole Vault
Logbook

As a collegiate vaulter, I ritually wrote down everything regarding my vault during a practice or competition. This included the pole I was jumping with, where I ran from on the runway, what height or drill I was attempting, and more.

I created this custom journal for pole vaulters to track every mechanical detail of their vault, capture goals, and reflect on their training and performance.
