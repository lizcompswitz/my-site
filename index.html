Coaches
Box.

Reimagining the coaches box.

You're probably thinking of the uncomfortable and often poorly placed area coaches are meant to stand while at a meet. As a college pole vault coach, I've argued with officials and meet managers and complained to colleagues about these spaces countless times. I have experienced the frustration of trying to effectively coach my athletes while officials bark at me to get in the box.

Finally, a coaches box worth standing in.

In an effort to reclaim the space, at least metaphorically, I created CoachesBox (the digital tool) to be the exact opposite of coaching boxes (the physical space). CoachesBox gives structure for a sport that lives in and outside the lines. It's a space for T&F coaches to build and evolve their training programs, track their athletes' progress, capture program goals, and, ultimately, create a better athlete experience.

Built from the ground up by a coach who likes to experiement and color outside the lines but understands the importance of structure, CoachesBox makes it easier than ever to create and sustain effective T&F training programs.

Features

Build structured training blocks, cycles, and individual training

Track athlete progress across events and seasons

Define and measure program goals

Designed for track & field — our sport is specific and your tools should be too

Pole Vault
Logbook

As a collegiate vaulter, I ritually wrote down everything regarding my vault during a practice or competition. This included the pole I was jumping with, where I ran from on the runway, what height or drill I was attempting, and more. I created this custom journal for pole vaulters to track every mechanical detail of their vault, capture goals, and reflect on their training and performance.
